This is a **beginner-level HR Analytics project** created using **Microsoft Excel** to analyze employee attrition.

The purpose of this project is to understand **why employees leave an organization** and identify patterns based on:
- Department
- Age
- Gender
- Job satisfaction
- Work-life balance
- Salary
- Experience level

All insights are created using **Excel formulas, helper columns, pivot tables, KPIs, and dashboards**.

---

##  Project Goals
- Calculate overall employee attrition rate
- Identify departments with high attrition
- Analyze attrition by age group and gender
- Understand how job satisfaction and work-life balance affect attrition
- Study the impact of salary and experience on employee turnover
- Build a clean **HR KPI Dashboard in Excel**

---

- **Total Employees:** 1,470

## Key Insights

### ðŸ“Œ Overall Attrition
- The company has an attrition rate of **16%**, meaning a noticeable number of employees are leaving.

### ðŸ“Œ Department-wise Attrition
- **Research & Development** has the highest attrition.
- **Sales** is the second highest.
- **Human Resources** has the lowest attrition.

### ðŸ“Œ Demographic Insights
- Attrition is higher among **male employees** compared to female employees.
- Employees **below 30 years of age** show the highest attrition, indicating early-career turnover.

### ðŸ“Œ Job Satisfaction & Work-Life Balance
- Attrition is not limited to employees with low satisfaction.
- Employees with **high and very high satisfaction** also leave, suggesting other reasons like career growth or salary.

### ðŸ“Œ Salary & Experience Impact
- Most attrition occurs among:
  - **Low-income employees**
  - **Junior-level employees**
- Mid-level and senior employees show much lower attrition.
